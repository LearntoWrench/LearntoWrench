2. Car Doctor 101 

Getting into car repair and struggling to find what the problem is? Driving around with a squealing sound? Is your car overheating? Leaking oil? Certain features no longer working? Spending money on parts without a conclusive diagnosis?

Learn to Wrench can teach the diagnostic process a technician performs for each specific scenario and vehicle. Depending on the symptom you are experiencing, one class will look much different than another. In some instances the process may involve removing parts, in others it may not be necessary for example. If for any reason LTW teaches the process and the problem is still not identified, Learn to Wrench will not charge any money for the class. That is how confident we are in teaching high quality and conclusive diagnostic classes that work. 

LTW can perform can teach many diagnostic processes. Refer to the list below to get a feel for the skills we can teach.

How to:
1. Check diagnostic codes in the car (1996+).
2. Identify charging issues.
3. Identify engine performance issues.
4.Identify overheating issues.
5. Identify where any leaks are coming from.
6.Identify no start issues.
7. Identify electrical problems. 
8. Pin point misfires. 
9. Identify concerning driveability symptoms in the steering, suspension, or driveline of the vehicle.
10. Perform a compression test.

Just like with repairs, we can teach most inquiries. Please get in touch with anything you are struggling with!

That being said, there are certain functions we cannot perform/ teach.

LTW cannot 
-Perform smoke tests.
-Teach any AC diagnostic work.
-Road test or ride passenger in any non street legal vehicle. 

3. The DIY Plunge   

Do you want to be as self-reliant as possible when it comes to car repair? Are you an adventurer who isn't afraid to take a plunge into the unknown? The DIY plunge class is for people who want to learn the entire process, including the diagnosis and the repair. This is usually a two class process. One class for the diagnosis, and another one to install the part. We teach students everything, including how to order parts for their vehicle. Getting parts may be immediate if we can find them locally, or it may take days to order for them to come in the mail. This process is a rewarding one but takes patience and an open mind! Learn to Wrench FAQ

What is Learn to Wrench?

Learn to Wrench is an educational service that teaches automotive diagnosis, repair, and maintenance. Students inquire about specific jobs they would like to learn on their car. Each class is unique and tailored to the specific repair(s) and diagnosis a student wants or needs to learn at that point in time. 

Learn to Wrench makes car repair education more accessible, and more student centered while also being more convenient. Inquire for a class for when YOU are free and have time to learn instead of when a class schedule says so. 

Where can I take a Learn to Wrench class?

Learn to Wrench mobilly teaches in most towns and cities near Amherst, MA, 01002. Learn to Wrench can come to your location to teach classes on most vehicles. 

How does Learn to Wrench work?

1. Inquire for a class with our form, located in the top menu or here.

2. Learn to Wrench will get back to you with a determination if we are able to teach the class, a price and an estimated amount of time it will take to teach the requested jobs. Along with this will be a time and place based off the answers on the inquiry form. 

3. Once you get a response from LTW confirm the class or you can decline the class as well if you have any issues with our quote. 

4. If you accept the class we will meet and get working at the location time and date we agreed on!

Who supplies the parts if they are necessary?

Students supply the parts. Learn to Wrench can assist sourcing and getting the right parts if necessary! 

What if something goes poorly during a repair?

Before taking a class with us, understand that we cannot make a bad circumstance like horribly rusted bolts that  will break become clean for example. We do our absolute best to teach every learner on any car they have, but in some situations the car may be so far gone with corrosion that it's not even worth touching the bolts and nuts for example. In these situations LTW will not be able to continue teaching  a repair, or even start teaching a repair if everything is likely going to break and cause even worse problems. 

If any other weird and unlikely circumstances happen while working on a car we will do our best to help you resolve them immediately, but in some circumstances it may be just like at the shop where sometimes things break unexpectedly just because you were in there servicing something else. Again all these unwanted issues are more and more likely with age. In these circumstances which has only happened once so far, we can teach another class at another time to remedy the problem, or you could opt to have a professional service the car if you are afraid of your car causing too many problems while in the driveway. If a class is cut short for any reason we only expect payment for how far into the process you were. If something bad happens 40% of the way into the repair we only expect 40% of the class quote as the repair may not be completable at that time.
